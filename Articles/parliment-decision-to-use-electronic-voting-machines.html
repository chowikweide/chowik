Is Parliament's decision to use Electronic Voting Machines in the next Election lead to more worse election results?

By Chowik Weide
on 6/15/22

On the 17th of November, 2021, the parliament of Pakistan passed a bill that allow the amendment of the style of voting. Therefore, from the next elections, Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) will be used in the next elections. The regular voting style is not considered satisfactory for pure voting. This is a valid reason for the amendment of voting style, but it's not guaranteed that the new way of voting will have fair results. There are a lot of objections by the opposition to the decision. Therefore, the parliament’s decision to use electronic voting machines will lead to worse election results than the manual voting system.

Electronic voting machines require biometric verification. According to FIA, NADRA was hacked last week, which caused all of Pakistanis biometric data to be leaked to anonymous people. Since these kinds of stuff may usually be available on the dark web for bitcoins, so anyone can buy and misuse it for any purpose. Therefore, it’s clear that Pakistanis’ biometric data is unsafe and the EVMs can be tampered with this data to produce the desired result, which may lead to unfair election results.

The minister of science, Shibli Faraz, opened a challenge for everyone to hack the EVM, and 1 Million PKR will be awarded to the person who will hack the EVM. Nevertheless, According to BBC news: "A hacker girl, Beatrice Atobatele, gained unauthorized access of EVM." It was hard for her to crack the voting machines which are usually used in the U.S.

However, she found ways to manipulate EVM data. Therefore, one can conclude that EVM is not that secure, and can be used to produce unfair election results.

According to a scholarly research report: "Manipulating voting machines is a concrete, easy-to-understand method for tampering with elections. But is there an actual danger of such attacks succeeding in the United States? Based on recent experiences in other countries, the evolution of cyber-attacks over the last decade, and current vulnerabilities in our system, the answer is yes." This report explicitly states that hacking voting machine is feasible if we seek through recent cyber-attacks to malfunction EVMs.

The same report also revealed the fact that tampering with electronic machines is not the only part of manipulating the whole election result. Since data can also be hacked from the second node of the server, not only the voting machine. In simpler words, the output of each vote count which is being sent to the server can also tamper which makes it easier for a hacker to malfunction the electronic voting system without having physical access to it.

Furthermore, In the traditional voting system, voters know whom they are voting for. In case, if someone forces one to give a vote, then at least one can report this to ECP. But, the EVMs will transfer the data digitally. Manipulating digital data is not that hard for skilled people, and they are also untraceable. In case of any suspected result of EVM, the hacker will remain anonymous. Therefore, EVMs may not give transparent results when someone will tamper with the EVM.

Other than the insecurity of EVM, there is one more crucial thing to argue the fact that EVMs are a worse option as compared to the traditional voting style. Each voting machine costs up to 5,00,000 PKR. According to a report published in 2018, there are 85,000 polling stations all over Pakistan. Then, the total budget of only EVMs will be around 43 Billion PKR. If we seek through the current economy of Pakistan, so it does not sound a good idea to use EVMs in the election if we compare it to less expensive manual voting.

The Election Commission of Pakistan raised 37 objections regarding the parliament's decision of using EVMs. According to the DAWN news, "The ECP also referred to various other issues linked with the use of EVM, including lack of ballot secrecy, lack of capacity at all levels and lack of ensuring security and chain of custody for the machines at rest and during transportation. It also pointed out that there would be no evidence available in case of an election dispute. The ECP noted that data integration and configuration issues may crop up due to court orders at the eleventh hour regarding a change in the ballot paper." In the above statement, the writer, Iftikhar Ahsan Khan, mainly talked about the concerns regarding electronic voting machines. Its vulnerability is not uncommon and can not be ignored as it will be the future of the next four years of Pakistan. Therefore, using EVMs without ensuring that it won't lead to unfair results is not a good option at all.

According to cyber security experts, ethical hackers, and bug hunters, the EVMs are hackable. Undoubtedly, they are hard to hack, but there is some probability that someone can misuse them. When there is a probability of misuse of EVMs, then saying that EVM will always provide fair results is not justified. Moreover, according to past stories, EVMs were being hacked for manipulating election results. In addition to it, the economy of Pakistan is also not that good to afford each electronic voting machine for such a big number of polling stations. Therefore, it is true to conclude that EVMs may lead to worst election results than ever!
